ELL4919 COMPARATIVE ENGLISH LITERATURES(Comparative English Literatures) 
In this course, we will explore the idea of the apocalypse/post-apocalypse in English literature through the 
ages. Our main reading will be a trio of powerful contemporary novels (Mitchell, Foer, Ozeki) that treat these 
topics within defining events of our generation. In between, we will take a step back into history, reading 
eighteenth and nineteenth century selections (Defoe, Malthus, Shelley, and Jefferies.)

ELL4921 지혜와삶의예술(Wisdom and the Art of Life) 
In the past, it was common for people to read classic literature to gain insights on how to live their lives. 
Monks and courtiers wrote treatises and maxims giving advice on how to excel in society, while the great 
French novelists served as guides for young people from the provinces seeking to make their fortune in 
Paris. Ancient writers provided reflections on how to live in harmony with one's circumstances. In this class, 
we will examine the literature of wisdom, both ancient and modern, and take up the question of how reading 
literature can deepen, enrich, and improve one's life in modern society. 
ELL4922 LITERATURE AND REVOLUTION (2021년 개설) 
This course examines the literary representations and philosophical reflections on the French Revolution of 1789 
and the Revolution of 1848. We will examine the breakdown of monarchical authority, the Reign of Terror, and 
the dictatorships that brought an end to the chaos brought about by revolution. We will read and discuss the 
works of Charles Dickens, Gustave Flaubert, Alexis de Tocqueville, Joseph de Maistre, Thomas Carlyle, and 
others. 
ELL4923 서구문명의쇠퇴와몰락(The Decline and Fall of Western Civilization) 
The class will examine the ideas of decline and collapse that have become increasingly widespread in 
the West. We will examine some of the most influential and compelling accounts of the fall of 
civilization, such as those of Edward Gibbon and Oswald Spengler. We will also examine literary and 
cinematic efforts to imagine the collapse and its aftermath.
